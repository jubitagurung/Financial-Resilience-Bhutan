Why I Built SmartBudget Bhutan

I am currently a 3rd-year, 2nd-semester student of Gedu College of Business Studies, working as an intern at Bhutan Data Scientist Pvt. Ltd. (BDS). I am working on a project focused on financial resilience at the household level in urban Bhutan.

Growing up and studying in Bhutan, I personally experienced both the importance and the challenges of managing money wisely at a young age. As students, most of us do not have a regular source of income. We rely on pocket money from our parents and a small monthly stipend of around Nu. 3,000.

Like many others, I often found myself running out of money in the middle of the month. Even small needs of Nu. 100 or Nu. 200 became difficult to manage when my bank balance reached zero.

This experience inspired me to create SmartBudget Bhutan — a platform to help students, employees, and individuals build stronger financial habits and make better financial decisions.

Through this website, I am to promote financial awareness by providing practical tools such as budget planner templates, basic budgeting methods, interactive quizzes, and other simple learning resources.

Financial resilience is not about how much you earn
it is about how well you manage what you have.

Budgeting Principles

Pay Yourself FirstTransfer savings immediately when you receive your salary treat it like a non-negotiable bill.
Track Every NgultrumSmall daily expenses like tea and snacks add up. Log them weekly to spot leaks.
Avoid Lifestyle CreepWhen income rises, maintain your current lifestyle and increase savings rate instead.
Separate AccountsKeep savings in a separate account to reduce temptation to spend it.

About This Project

Research · Data · Impact

Project Background

I am an intern at Bhutan Data Scientist Pvt. Ltd. (BDS), working on financial resilience at the household level in urban Bhutan.

This project analyzes real household data to understand income, expenses, savings behaviour, and financial stress points among Bhutanese families with the goal of making insights accessible and actionable.

Objectives

  • Identify key financial challenges in urban Bhutanese households
  • Visualize income, expense, and saving patterns clearly
  • Provide practical, data-informed budgeting recommendations
  • Promote long-term financial stability and resilience
  • Make complex financial insights accessible to everyone

Tools & Technology

This project uses a modern data-to-web pipeline to turn raw household data into clear, actionable insights.
